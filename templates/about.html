About Odd Future


Meet Odd Future



Stage Name: Tyler, The Creator

Given Name: Tyler Gregory Okonma

Date of Birth: Mar 06, 1991 - Age 25

Hometown: Los Angeles, California

Fun Facts: He attended twelve years of grade school at twelve different schools.
                  He taught himself how to play the piano at age fourteen.
                  He suffers from asthma and brings his inhaler to performances.

About Tyler: When the Los Angeles based hip-hop collective Odd Future gained serious worldwide attention in 2011, it was due to OF frontman Tyler, The Creator’s creativity. He dropped the music video for “Yonkers”, the first single off of his first studio album, and ever since he and his fellow OF members have been at the center of the hip-hop world. Overall, Tyler has released two studio albums, Goblin (2011) and Wolf (2013) as well as his first solo project, a free album called “Bastard” that he released in 2009. He was also instrumental in Odd Future’s four collective projects.



Stage Name: Earl Sweatshirt

Given Name: Thebe Neruda Kgositsile

Date of Birth: Feb 24, 1994 - Age 22

Hometown: Los Angeles, California

Fun Facts: He recorded his first, critically-acclaimed mixtape, "Earl," when he was only sixteen.
                  Earl's father is Keorapetse Kgositsile, a famous South-African poet.
                  Earl is best friends with Tyler the Creator, the de-facto leader of Odd Future.

About Earl: Earl Sweatshirt became famous for his music before anyone was sure who he was, or more specifically, where he was. A core member of the Odd Future Wolf Gang collective, Earl was conspicuously absent when Odd Future first began to gain notoriety. It was puzzling because he was referenced by other Odd Future members constantly, although always through the same mystic (catch) phrase, "Free Earl.".



Stage Name: Frank Ocean

Given Name: Christopher Edwin Breaux

Date of Birth: Oct 28, 1987 - Age 28

Hometown: New Orleans, Louisiana

Fun Facts: Ocean attended the University of New Orleans and the University of Louisiana.
                  Ocean relocated to Los Angeles after Hurricane Katrina.
                  His debut album, Channel ORANGE, reminds him of the summer he first fell in love with a man.

About Frank: Frank Ocean is one of, if not the most recognizable R&B crooner of the new generation. After joining the Odd Future Wolf Gang Kill Them All collective in 2010 and receiving critical praise for his debut mixtape Nostalgia, Ultra, the New Orleans native skyrocketed to recognition, touring internationally and appearing on Jay Z and Kanye West’s collaborative album Watch The Throne (on the standout cut "No Church In The Wild").
